Best design portfolio inspiration sites in 2026

My go-to design portfolio inspiration sites, complete with detailed breakdowns to help inspire your portfolio website design.

Adham Dannaway Last updated 1 January 2026

Whether you’re a UX designer, product designer, illustrator, brand designer, or web designer, creating your design portfolio will likely be the most fun and most challenging project you’ll work on in your design career. Getting started can feel overwhelming, especially when you’re unsure where to begin or struggling with creative block. One of the best ways to get inspired is by exploring portfolios from other designers. After decades working as a product designer, I’ve compiled a list of my favourite design portfolio inspiration sites.

While you can often filter general web design inspiration sites by “portfolio” type, there aren’t many dedicated to showcasing portfolio websites only. In this article, I’ve evaluated the top design portfolio inspiration sites based on their update frequency, search and filter functionality, variety of examples, newsletters, and social media presence. 

To simplify things, I’ve also highlighted my 3 favourites. These design portfolio inspiration sites are sure to spark your creativity and help you craft a portfolio that truly stands out.

What is a design portfolio website?

A design portfolio website is an online platform where designers showcase their work, skills, and expertise. It acts as a digital résumé and gallery, allowing potential clients or employers to view a designer’s design projects and understand their skills, style, and experience. 

Your portfolio website gives you a chance to freely express yourself  without meddling clients or hovering art directors getting in the way. That’s why portfolio websites are often the most unique and interesting websites out there on the internet.

Here’s my own portfolio site that I created back in 2008. My goal was to create something unique to help me stand out from the crowd. It turned out that lots of designers liked the concept and were “heavily inspired” by it.

A design portfolio website typically includes:

  • Examples of past projects or case studies.
  • An “about” section detailing the designer’s background and approach.
  • Contact information to allow others to get in touch via email or follow the designer on social media.
  • Additional features like testimonials, blog posts, or downloadable résumés.

A well-crafted design portfolio website not only highlights technical and creative skills but also reflects the designer’s personal brand, making it a critical tool for career growth in the UX, product, and web design industry.

Quick portfolio website design tips

Follow these tips to create a portfolio website that effectively showcases your work and skills, tells your story, and lands you the clients or design jobs you’re after.

  1. Define your purpose: Decide whether your portfolio will focus on attracting clients, showcasing skills for jobs, or sharing personal projects, and design accordingly.
  2. Focus on your best work: Feature only your strongest projects to leave a lasting impression. Quality generally beats quantity in a portfolio website.
  3. Show your personality: Infuse your site with unique design elements, colour schemes, or typography that reflect your personal brand and creativity. This is actually what helped my portfolio website get featured in blog posts and galleries, as it was so different to others out there at the time.
  4. Include an “About” page: People connect with people. Share your background, skills, and design philosophy to add a personal touch and connect with your audience.
  5. Provide detailed case studies: Go beyond images by explaining your design process, challenges, and solutions to demonstrate your expertise. Be sure to highlight how your solution achieved the goal using metrics or customer feedback.
  6. Build trust with social proof: Add testimonials, reviews, trust badges, or client logos to reassure visitors of your credibility and reliability.
  7. Highlight your role: Clearly specify your contributions in collaborative projects to give visitors a clear understanding of your skills and involvement.
  8. Use high-quality visuals: Ensure your work is presented with large, high-resolution images or mockups to showcase your designs in the best light. Demonstration videos can also work well to show animations and interactions.
  9. Add a clear call-to-action: Make it easy for potential clients or employers to contact you by including a prominent call to action. Contact forms work well and help ensure you collect the information you need to provide a quote for client projects.
  10. Keep it updated: Regularly add new projects, update your skills, and remove outdated work to ensure your portfolio remains relevant. This is the one that many of us, including myself, struggle with. 

3 best design portfolio inspiration sites

One of the things that sets portfolio websites apart from others is that you can run wild with your creativity. I love to see the many different ways creative people present themselves and their work. The following design portfolio inspiration sites are my favourite of the bunch. Each of them excels across most of the judging criteria, so I’m sure they’ll help inspire you while creating or updating your portfolio website design. I’ve focussed on showcasing free sites to ensure they’re accessible to all.

Killer Portfolio

Killer Portfolio is my favourite free design portfolio inspiration site because it’s different from the others. Every portfolio featured on the site comes with commentary and numerous screenshots to help you understand what makes each portfolio design special.

There’s basically a small case study for each portfolio to unlock the secrets of what makes a killer portfolio. Curated by Mike Harmer, you can really tell that they have a passion for portfolio website design and know how to find particularly beautiful and unique portfolios. The extra effort Mike puts into each of the featured portfolio sites sets this resource ahead of the rest. 

The site highlights diverse portfolio styles, providing a wealth of inspiration for designers looking to create their own standout online presence. Its clean layout and well-organised structure make browsing seamless and enjoyable. I find myself just sitting at my desk with my cup of tea browsing through each portfolio one at a time. It’s fun to see the weird and wonderful things designers create when they have full creative freedom.

Unique features include:

  • Case studies for each portfolio: Rather than simply displaying a screenshot of the portfolio website, each portfolio comes with commentary and numerous screenshots to help you understand what makes it a killer portfolio. 
  • Beautifully curated selection: The curator, Mike Harmer, has particularly good taste and selects only the most impressive portfolio designs to showcase.
  • Clean, distraction-free interface: The site itself is designed beautifully with some clever visual details, making it a pleasure to browse. You can easily focus on the portfolios themselves without any unnecessary clutter.

Visit Killer Portfolio

Foleo

Foleo showcases a wide variety of creative design portfolios. It highlights the best work from designers across multiple disciplines, offering a rich source of inspiration for creating your own portfolio. The website focuses on high-quality, visually striking portfolios to help you stand out in the crowd.

I like the taller than usual preview thumbnails that allow you to quickly browse portfolio designs. I’d love to see more search filters added in the future though, as they’re currently limited to a handful based on the portfolio website style. Each portfolio site comes with a large desktop screenshot along with details regarding the platform used to build the site and the fonts used. The screenshot isn’t full screen, so you’ll need to leave the site to view the full page. 

This is one of the only dedicated design portfolio inspiration sites I could find with a newsletter. I like newsletters, as they keep you up to date with the latest designs without you needing to remember to visit the site. This is also one of the only regularly updated free portfolio inspiration sites out there, as many of the others unfortunately no longer seem to be maintained.

Unique features include:

  • Diverse portfolio collection: Features work from various creative fields including UX, product design, and brand design ensuring broad inspiration.
  • Modern design examples: Updated regularly, it showcases portfolios with innovative layouts, bold typography, and interactive elements.
  • Category filter: Filter portfolios based on portfolio style including minimal, dark, and clean.
  • Regular updates: Continuously adds fresh content to keep the collection relevant and inspiring.
  • Monthly newsletter: Get portfolio inspiration emailed to you monthly. 

Visit Foleo

Bestfolios

Bestfolios is a comprehensive design portfolio inspiration site showcasing some of the most impressive design portfolios from designers in a range of creative fields. It features portfolios across UX design, graphic design, motion design, industrial design, and UX research. The site doesn’t just feature portfolio designs either, it also includes resumes and specific examples of impressive design case studies. 

This curated collection of portfolios is a great place to gain valuable insights into different design styles, techniques, and trends. Each portfolio site comes with a full page screenshot along with similar portfolios you may also like. There’s even an email newsletter to make sure you get your weekly dose of portfolio design inspiration. 

Bestfolios is an awesome resource for designers looking to create a standout portfolio or even if you simply want to see what cool and crazy things other designers are doing to present themselves and their work.

Unique features include:

  • Wide variety of design disciplines: Features portfolios across UX design, graphic design, motion design, industrial design, and UX research, catering to different creative fields.
  • Curated collection: Focuses on high-quality portfolios, ensuring the featured work represents industry-leading talent.
  • Features case studies and resumes: Bestfolios also showcases specific case studies from portfolios as well as resumes.
  • Frequent updates: Regularly adds fresh portfolios, keeping the collection relevant and up to date.

Visit Bestfolios

Other great design portfolio inspiration sites

The top 3 design portfolio inspiration sites might be sufficient for most, but if you’re keen for more design portfolio inspiration, I’ve got you covered. Here’s a regularly updated list of my other favourite sites for portfolio inspiration.

Wall of Portfolios

Wall of Portfolios stands out from most design portfolio inspiration sites, as it includes a full interactive website preview of each portfolio site. Most sites only provide a screenshot preview. This means you can browse hundreds of portfolios without ever leaving the site, which is pretty handy.

The quality of portfolio designs featured on the site is varied, as the collection includes many junior designers eager to get their name out there and be featured on the homepage for the portfolio of the month award. There are still lots of creative designs being showcased which is great to see.

A short profile is created for each designer who submits their portfolio. The profile includes a little bit about them, where they’re located, how many years work experience they have, as well as the companies they’ve worked for. Many of the portfolios featured on the site were created by designers from India, but submissions are open to anyone. 

Unique features include:

  • Diverse portfolio collection: Features portfolios from designers in various disciplines, including UX/UI, product design, graphic design, and branding.
  • Quick portfolio previews: Displays full interactive website previews of portfolios, enabling you to browse and explore without leaving the site.
  • X social feed: The X social channel is especially good to stay up to date with the latest designs, as it’s updated regularly and features video previews.
  • Community focus: Celebrates individual designers and their work, creating a platform for recognition and inspiration.

Visit Wall of Portfolios

Pafolios

Pafolios is a great design portfolio inspiration site that showcases outstanding design portfolios from across the web. One thing that sets it apart from other portfolio collections is its super tall thumbnails, which allow you to quickly browse through portfolio designs. If you need a bit more detail than the thumbnail provides, there’s also a full page screenshot for each portfolio.

The quality of featured portfolios is high and you can filter them based on the type of designer that created them. Filters include UX & product design, brand design, writers, illustrators, artists, and photographers. There’s also a weekly email newsletter to keep you inspired. Definitely an excellent resource for discovering exceptional design talent and improving your own portfolio standards.

Unique features include:

  • High-quality curation: Showcases only the most creative and polished design portfolios, ensuring every entry is worth exploring.
  • Wide range of design fields: Includes portfolios from various disciplines, such as UX/UI design, graphic design, and illustration.
  • Minimalist interface: Features a clean and straightforward layout, allowing you to browse portfolios without distractions.
  • Tall thumbnail previews: Extra tall thumbnail images allow you to quickly browse through portfolio designs before seeing the full page screenshot in more detail.
  • Case studies: Top design case studies are also featured and include some inspiring case studies from Lonely Planet, Apple Music, and Uber.

Visit Pafolios

Pretty Folio

Prettyfolio is all about showcasing beautiful and creative design portfolios from top designers around the world. While the site hasn’t been updated recently, it celebrates unique and eye-catching designs, making it a great place to find inspiration for your own portfolio. The search filters are limited, but the full page screenshots speed up the browsing experience. 

Unique features include:

  • Beautiful designs: Focuses on high quality, visually stunning portfolios that grab attention and inspire creativity.
  • Varied styles: Features a mix of designs, from sleek and simple to bold and artistic.
  • Full page screenshots: Full page screenshots of each portfolio website speed up the browsing experience.

Visit Pretty Folio

Personal Sites

Personal Sites is a thoughtfully curated collection of unique portfolio websites, offering a treasure trove of inspiration for designers looking to craft portfolios with personality and flair. It was originally built and curated by well known designer, Andy Bell, but judging by the style of the featured portfolios, the collection hasn’t been updated in a while. Luckily, there’s still much on offer, as a well designed website can last the test of time, especially if its design fundamentals are solid. 

The design of this portfolio inspiration site is super minimal. It’s basically just a feed of small thumbnails that link off to the portfolio website itself. No search filters, detailed screenshots, or newsletters. I guess that’s all you really need in the end.

Unique features include:

  • Curated selection: Originally built and curated by well known designer, Andy Bell, every featured site is handpicked to ensure only the most creative and interesting personal websites make the cut.
  • Minimalist design: The clean and distraction-free layout makes for a simple browsing experience, although some filters and large screenshots would be helpful.

Visit Personal Sites

Portfolio Project

When it comes to design portfolio inspiration sites, Portfolio Project is quite an enigma. I’m not sure who created it and whether or not it’s still maintained, but it’s still worth mentioning, as it features some unique portfolio websites from professionals across a range of creative fields. Whether you’re starting from scratch or refining an existing portfolio, this site offers plenty of ideas to make your work shine.

Unique features include:

  • Diverse portfolio styles: Features a wide range of portfolio designs, from minimal and modern to bold and experimental, offering inspiration for all tastes.
  • Clean browsing experience: The simple and intuitive design ensures you can quickly browse and find inspiration without clutter or distractions.

Visit Portfolio Project
