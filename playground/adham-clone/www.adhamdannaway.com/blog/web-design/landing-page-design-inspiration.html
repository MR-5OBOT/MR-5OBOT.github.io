Best landing page design inspiration sites in 2026

My go-to landing page design inspiration sites with detailed breakdowns to help inspire you on your next landing page design.

Adham Dannaway Last updated 1 January 2026

Starting a landing page design can be challenging, especially when creative block hits. One of the best ways to overcome this hurdle is by exploring other websites for inspiration. After decades of designing landing pages, I’ve compiled a list of my favourite landing page design inspiration sites.

With so many options available, it can be tricky to know where to start. That’s why I’ve compared the top landing page design inspiration sites based on their update frequency, search and filter functionality, website previews, newsletters, and social media presence. To make it even easier, I’ve highlighted my top three picks for landing page inspiration. 

All of these landing page design inspiration sites are completely free to use too, which makes them accessible to everyone. Hopefully, these resources will spark your creativity and make your landing page design process faster and smoother.

What is a landing page?

A landing page is a standalone web page designed to guide visitors toward a specific goal or action. Unlike a full website, which typically includes multiple pages and navigation options, a landing page focuses on delivering a clear and targeted message.

It’s often used in marketing campaigns to drive conversions, such as signing up for a newsletter, downloading a resource, or purchasing a product. Here’s an example of a landing page I created for my UI design book.

Key features of a landing page:

  • Single goal: The entire page is designed around one call-to-action (CTA) that you’re encouraging the user to take.
  • Minimal navigation: It reduces distractions by limiting or removing external links and navigation menus.
  • Focused content: The messaging is clear, concise, and aligned with the audience’s intent.
  • Optimised design: Visual elements and layouts are crafted to highlight the call to action and guide user attention.

Quick landing page design tips

Some practical landing page design tips to help you design effective and high-converting landing pages that deliver results.

  1. Define a clear goal: Focus on a single, specific objective, such as lead generation, sales, or sign-ups. Avoid overwhelming visitors with multiple calls to action.
  2. Create a compelling headline: Use a strong, attention-grabbing headline that clearly communicates the value of your offering within seconds.
  3. Use an impactful hero section: Include high-quality visuals or videos paired with concise messaging to immediately engage visitors.
  4. Design for simplicity: Keep the layout clean and uncluttered, with a logical flow that guides visitors toward the desired action.
  5. Optimise your call to action (CTA): Make your CTA stand out with a prominent button, clear language, and contrasting colours to encourage clicks.
  6. Build trust with social proof: Add testimonials, reviews, trust badges, or client logos to reassure visitors of your credibility and reliability.
  7. Incorporate persuasive copy: Highlight benefits over features, using concise and persuasive language that resonates with your target audience.
  8. Test and optimise: Regularly run A/B tests on elements like headlines, CTAs, and imagery to improve conversion rates.
  9. Minimise distractions: Limit navigation options and unnecessary links to keep the focus on the primary goal of the landing page.
  10. Add contact details or live chat options: Provide clear ways for visitors to get in touch or resolve queries to build confidence in taking action.

3 best landing page design inspiration sites

With so many awesome landing page design inspiration sites, it was hard to narrow it down to just 3. Each of these 3 sites excels across most of the judging criteria, so I’m sure they’ll help inspire you while working on your next landing page design.

One Page Love

Created back in 2008 by the king of landing page design, Rob Hope, One Page Love is my favourite landing page design inspiration site. The site features a comprehensive collection of over 8,000 creative and high-quality one-page websites. I was lucky enough to have my UI design book’s landing page featured on the site.

One of the main things that makes One Page Love such a powerful tool for finding landing page design inspiration is the comprehensive tagging system. Each and every website is meticulously tagged based on its features, industry, typefaces, and platform. The large thumbnails also make it super quick to browse layouts at a glance.

Along with landing page design inspiration, the site also includes templates, tutorials, and helpful resources for crafting impactful single-page websites. You can easily stay in the loop via numerous social media channels and the bi-weekly newsletter is especially helpful. Definitely a handy resource for landing page design inspiration.

Unique features include:

  • Focus on one-page websites: You’ll only find single-page website designs on this site, which often make the most powerful landing pages due to their laser focus and simplicity.
  • Huge collection: With over 8,000 curated single page websites, this is one of the largest collections I know of.
  • Extensive variety: The collection spans multiple industries and styles, from personal portfolios to corporate landing pages.
  • Additional resources: It offers templates, design tips, and tutorials, making it more than just an inspiration site.
  • Comprehensive tagging: The website’s categorisation system helps you quickly filter and find landing pages that suit specific design needs or themes.
  • High-quality curation: Low quality sites simply won’t make the cut. Each featured site is carefully selected, ensuring a consistently inspiring and practical resource.

Visit One Page Love

Lapa Ninja

Showcasing over 6,000 landing page designs, Lapa Ninja is one of the most comprehensive landing page design inspiration sites on the web. It’s a vibrant and well-organised site with a diverse collection of high-quality landing pages. It aims to help designers and developers find ideas for their next project, featuring landing pages from various industries, each selected for its unique and creative approach. 

I love the extra tall thumbnails, as they allow you to see a good chunk of the landing page at a glance when browsing. The search filters are also super handy, as you can filter by a large range of categories, typefaces, and colours.

Lapa Ninja are also generous with their website screenshots, displaying multiple full page screenshots of the entire website. You literally never need to leave the site when browsing for inspiration. There’s also lots of social media channels to follow for daily inspiration, and the weekly newsletter is great too.

Unique features include:

  • Wide variety of landing page designs: It features an extensive selection from different sectors, ensuring inspiration for a range of projects.
  • Regularly updated content: The site frequently adds new designs, keeping the collection fresh and relevant.
  • Search filters: Search filters for industry, colour, and typeface allow for precise and efficient browsing.
  • Large thumbnails: Extra tall thumbnails allow you to see a good chunk of the landing page at a glance when browsing.
  • Generous screenshots: Multiple full page screenshots of the entire website means you don’t need to leave the site.
  • Large collection: With over 6,000 curated landing pages, this is one of the largest collections out there.

Visit Lapa Ninja

Landbook

Launched back in 2013, Landbook has been the go-to landing page design inspiration site for many designers for a while now and it’s easy to see why. It has a huge curated collection of some of the best landing page designs on the web. While it started off as a completely free resource, the team has recently introduced a paid version which unlocks some of the new “pro” features. This small fee helps them pay the bills and keep developing new features, which is very fair if you ask me.

The free version is pretty good and includes a bunch of search filters as well as full page screenshots for both desktop and mobile. It’s not just landing pages either, they’ve also curated collections for other website types including portfolios, blogs, and ecommerce stores. In addition to that, there are collections for different page types too, such as about pages, pricing pages, and sign up pages. 

The paid version unlocks access to more categories, components, and search filters. It unlocks mobile screenshot previews and also allows you to save inspiration to folders for better organisation. Another cool thing is that you can see older versions of each website or page to see how they’ve evolved over time. Definitely a handy resource when you’re looking to design a landing page and need some inspiration. If you don’t mind paying a small fee, the pro version is my pick of the bunch. 

Unique features include:

  • Huge variety of landing pages: Showcases hundreds of landing page designs across different industries and styles, giving you plenty of ideas to explore.
  • Powerful filtering: Lets you filter pages by component, colour, style, industry, type, desktop, and mobile, ensuring you find designs that match your goals quickly.
  • Daily updates: Frequently adds new designs to keep the inspiration flowing and up-to-date with the latest trends.
  • Desktop and mobile screenshots: Displays full-page screenshots of designs in both desktop and mobile orientation, so you don’t need to leave the site.
  • Bookmarking feature: Allows you to save your favourite designs for easy access later.

Visit Landbook

Other great landing page design inspiration sites

The top 3 landing page design inspiration sites might be sufficient for most, but if you’re keen for more, I’ve got you covered. Here’s a regularly updated list of my other favourite sites for landing page design inspiration.

Landing Gallery

Landing Gallery is a sleek and straightforward landing page design inspiration site dedicated to showcasing high-quality landing pages. It’s one of my favourite places to go on the web for landing page design inspiration for many reasons. 

What’s unique about Landing Gallery is that it contains full page screenshots for both the desktop and mobile versions of each landing page. If the website has multiple pages, the other pages are captured too. So, you never need to leave the site. You can also subscribe to the weekly newsletter if you prefer to get your fix of landing page design inspiration in your inbox. 

The filters are pretty simple and only allow you to filter by website type and platform, but that’s often all you need. Most of the time you’re looking for inspiration for a specific page you’re working on like a pricing page, contact page, or about page. 

Unique features include:

  • High-quality curation: The site focuses on showcasing well-crafted landing pages that stand out for their design and functionality.
  • Desktop and mobile screenshots: It’s really handy to be able to view both desktop and mobile views of each landing page. They’re full page screenshots too.
  • Wide range of styles and industries: It offers diverse examples from different sectors, making it suitable for inspiration across various projects.
  • Constantly refreshed content: New landing pages are added regularly, ensuring the gallery stays up-to-date with current design trends.
  • OG image: The OG (Open Graph) or social media image for each website is also showcased.

Visit Landing Gallery

I love the clean and simple design of the site, as it brings focus to the content. With over 700 landing page examples, it’s not the largest collection, but the quality is high. The filters are also limited, but I like that you can filter by page type and typeface. It’s also nice to have a full page screenshot, so you don’t need to leave the site. It would be great to have some social channels to follow or a newsletter to stay up to date.

Unique features include:

  • Exclusive focus on SaaS landing pages: It provides inspiration tailored specifically for SaaS products, addressing the unique challenges of this market.
  • High-conversion designs: The collection emphasises landing pages optimised for user engagement and conversion, offering practical ideas for effective design.
  • Category-based browsing: You can navigate and filter designs by page type such as pricing pages, testimonials pages, or about pages.
  • Full page screenshots: Most landing page design inspiration sites only contain short screenshots, so you need to leave the site to view the landing page. SaaS Landing Page contains full page screenshots.

Visit SaaS Landing Page

Landdding

Landdding is a curated landing page design inspiration site showcasing high-quality landing page designs. Rather than being driven by a judging panel, landing pages are submitted and voted on by a community of landing page enthusiasts. This makes it one of the more democratic landing page design inspiration sites. The top 3 landing pages each day are awarded a badge.

The search and category filter are just enough to allow you to hone in on a specific type of landing page for inspiration. While there isn’t an email newsletter or social channels to follow, you’ll find a unique selection of landing pages here that you might not see on other landing page design inspiration sites. 

Unique features include:

  • Unique landing page inspiration: Offers a curated collection of unique and high-quality landing page designs that you might not see elsewhere.
  • Category-based browsing: You can navigate and filter designs by page type such as pricing pages, testimonials pages, or about pages.
  • Constantly refreshed content: New landing pages are added daily, ensuring the gallery stays up-to-date with current design trends.

Visit Landdding

Single Serving Sites

Single Serving Sites is a unique landing page design inspiration site that showcases simple, single-purpose websites. The landing pages featured here are generally composed of a single page with a dedicated domain name. 

It celebrates the creativity and functionality of minimalist web design, highlighting sites that excel in solving one specific problem or delivering a focused message. This resource is perfect for designers and developers seeking inspiration for concise and impactful landing pages.

Unique features include:

  • Focus on single-purpose landing pages: Highlights single-purpose websites generally with a single page and a dedicated domain name.
  • Inspires minimalist design thinking: highlights landing pages that excel in solving one specific problem or delivering a focused message.
